Welcome!

My research focuses focus on the development of statistical methods and computational tools and software for modeling of diseases with a background that encompasses biomedical, clinical, quantitative studies, and analyzing genetics and genomics data. I have extensive experience in conducting field work and managing data in large field-based research operations, performing algorithmic computations and model development in for diseases spatial-temporal models. I am specifically focused on statistical solutions for analyzing and pre-processing (normalizing) high-throughput genomic and next-generation sequencing data to infer both accurate and novel biological insights. My Interests includes: Applied statistics, Biostatistics, Mathematical models, Spatial analysis, Genomics, Bioinformatics, Large-Scale & Multi-Omic Single-Cell Data Analysis, evaluating models for single-cell RNA-seq data, evaluating methods for single-cell trajectory inference, intersection of single-cell genomics, statistical computing and statistical methods in bioscience, and Statistical Analysis of Genetic Data.

What am I doing right now?

Currently, lecturing at the University. I am a part of computational’s research group, and we are mostly working with extensions (Bayesian) tree-based models and its many possible applications.

Apart from that, I like to spend my "free time" on my Youtube channel, cycling and traveling.
